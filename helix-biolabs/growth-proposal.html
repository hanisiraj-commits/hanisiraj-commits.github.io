03 · List Growth

Aggressive, tested, all three months.

Built and run in partnership with Alia. Continuous A/B testing across popups, forms, and incentives — every new subscriber drops straight into a live flow.

The continuous testing program

List growth is not a one-month sprint — it runs every week for all 90 days. Each week we ship a new test against the previous winner: popup copy, design, offer angle, timing trigger, exit-intent variant. The winners stay, the losers go.

Every new subscriber lands inside a working flow, so each test compounds: more subs into Welcome, more revenue from abandoned, more reputation signal for the domain. Setup carries through to Q2.

05 · Compliance

Where peptide programs break down.

These are the lines we work inside. Every flow, every campaign, every popup gets reviewed against this list before it ships.

Risk · FDA

"Research Use Only" is not a shield.

FDA has stated RUO labeling is "a ruse" when dosing, testimonials, or human-use cues appear. The March 2025 Final Guidance (enforceable Jan 2026) prohibits consumer-facing marketing of unapproved peptides.

Risk · GLP-1

GLP-1s are the highest-risk SKUs.

The Sept 2025 FDA "green list" import alert targets semaglutide, tirzepatide, retatrutide. 50+ warning letters have already gone out for comparative claims ("like Ozempic"). Most conservative copy here, always.

Risk · FTC

FTC overlay on every health claim.

Even if FDA never knocks, any "lose weight," "build muscle," "heal injuries," or "anti-aging" copy creates direct FTC exposure. Outcome language gets stripped before any send.

Rule · Creative

Content must look like a lab.

No dosing. No before/after. No testimonials with results. No comparisons to FDA-approved drugs. Lead with COAs, purity reports, vials, batch numbers, and research framing.

Rule · Platform

Omnisend AUP is the soft ceiling.

More permissive than Klaviyo, but not unlimited. A complaint rate above 0.3% or a single high-profile report can trigger review. We mitigate with double opt-in, aggressive sunset, and research-coded creative.

Rule · Site

Site-level compliance is the gate.

21+ age gate on entry. Double opt-in popup with Privacy and Terms in context. Country detection drives SMS opt-in. None of the above is optional — these are pre-flight checks before the first send goes out.

06 · SMS

Why US SMS stays off the table.

The toll-free denial wasn't a paperwork issue. It's structural — three reasons why the program failed verification, and what we run instead.

01

The product itself.

US carriers extend the SHAFT framework (sex, hate, alcohol, firearms, tobacco) to include drug-adjacent categories: prescription drugs, research chemicals, peptides, SARMs. Auto-rejected at the carrier level, regardless of RUO labelling.

02

Site & opt-in scrutiny.

TCR and Twilio review the site for marketing/promotional consent text, STOP and HELP language, visible Privacy and Terms, business phone, and a sample message. Multiple gaps were likely present in the application.

03

TCPA exposure.

Statutory damages run $500–$1,500 per message for unapproved-drug categories. Reading the denial generously: the carrier is shielding the brand from a class-action vector that's actively being pursued in this category.

What we do instead
  • Do not retry the US toll-free. Geo-suppress US numbers from all SMS sends in Omnisend.
  • Run SMS to non-US subscribers only — UK, EU, AU, CA — under CASL/GDPR-compliant frameworks. The existing list of 117 international subscribers is fully usable.
  • Layer non-US SMS into Checkout Abandonment in Month 3 with an A/B test against email-only.
