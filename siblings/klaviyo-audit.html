Key improvement areas

1. Three overlapping welcome flows are cannibalising each other
Critical Effort: Medium

Right now, a new subscriber triggers three separate live welcome flows in parallel: Welcome Series 2026 (10 emails), Welcome to Cozy Club (1 email + A/B test), and one of the three Candle People quiz-triggered flows (Calm / Clarity / Comfort). Two more welcome flows — Welcome Series – New and Welcome – L//S — sit in draft but still generated $5,069 in revenue, meaning at least one probably fires for some trigger condition.

The downstream consequence is in the data: Welcome Series 2026 has a 2.22% unsubscribe rate and Welcome to Cozy Club has 2.11%. Benchmark is 0.3%. These aren't bad emails individually — they're good emails being sent on top of each other to the same person in the same 48-hour window. People unsubscribe because they feel bombarded, not because any one message is poor.

What to fix:

  • Kill or merge duplicate welcome flows. One canonical welcome flow, full stop.
  • Use the quiz answer as a split inside that one flow — Email 1 stays generic, Emails 2+ swap content blocks based on Candle Person Type (Calm / Clarity / Comfort).
  • Consolidate the 10-email sequence to 4–5 messages. Any welcome flow with 10 emails has diminishing returns after Email 4.
  • Move the "Welcome to Cozy Club" loyalty content to Email 3 of the consolidated flow, not its own parallel flow.
Revenue impact: Expected drop in welcome unsub rate from 2.2% to <0.5% preserves ~4,800 subscribers/year who are currently unsubscribing in the first week. At ~$50 per subscriber lifetime email value, that's +$240K annualised — the single largest lever in this audit.
2. Back-in-Stock flow is dormant — despite 6 of 9 products being sold out
Critical Effort: Quick Win

Scraping siblings.co right now: six of nine refill scents are sold out or waitlisted (Yuzu Bloom, No. 04, Green Hus, Pistachio Milk, No. 05, Neroli Noir). Yet the Back In Stock Flow – New processed only 822 recipients in 180 days and generated $2,572 — with flow revenue dropping to $0 in both February and April.

This is a brand where scarcity is the product. Customers actively want to be told when their scent returns. The mechanism exists in Klaviyo via the Subscribed to Back in Stock metric (it's already firing — it's just not being routed through a robust flow).

What to fix:

  • Expand the single-email Back-in-Stock flow into a 3-touch sequence: (1) "It's back" immediate, (2) "Scents go fast — here's what's left" 24hr, (3) "Final chance" 72hr with inventory countdown if stock <100 units.
  • Verify the Back-in-Stock widget is deployed on every sold-out PDP. Siblings has more sold-out SKUs than most brands ever will — this is the scarcity flywheel.
  • Add a Klaviyo segment: "Back-in-Stock subscriber | Never purchased" and route them into a short nurture sequence between restocks.
Revenue impact: Conservatively 10× the current 822-recipient volume (at minimum) if the widget is deployed properly. At the current $3.04 RPR of this flow, scaling to 8,000 recipients/yr = +$24K annualised.
3. Abandoned Cart and Checkout flows are under-triggering
High Effort: Medium

The numbers don't add up: you sent 86 campaigns at ~26k recipients each over 180 days, meaning around 2.3M email sends — but the Abandoned Cart flow only reached 702 recipients and Abandoned Checkout reached 1,389. For a store doing 7,565 orders in the same period, the cart/checkout funnel should be producing far more flow entries than this.

Abandoned Cart also didn't generate any revenue until January 2026 — strongly suggesting the flow was rebuilt/switched on recently and the earlier version wasn't firing. The good news is when they do fire, they work: Abandoned Cart produces $2.33 RPR and Abandoned Checkout $1.68 RPR — both multiples above any campaign.

What to fix:

  • Audit the Klaviyo Started Checkout and Added to Cart events. Both are firing (confirmed in metrics inventory) but the flow filters may be excluding too much.
  • Check that Smart Sending is NOT enabled on Email 1 of either flow — this commonly skips 90%+ of events.
  • Tighten timing: Abandoned Checkout Email 1 should fire within 30 minutes (not 2+ hours), Abandoned Cart within 1 hour.
  • Split both flows for first-time vs returning customers. For a brand with subscription + refill LTV, returning customer carts deserve different messaging (social proof about retention) vs first-time (trust + product education).
Revenue impact: If event routing is fixed and the flow reaches even a modest 6,000 recipients over 180 days (~8× current volume) at the current RPR, that's +$28K annualised. Add a plain-text founder email as the final touch — commonly lifts recovery 15–25%.
4. No automated winback flow — 33k "lapsing engaged" profiles sitting idle
High Effort: Medium

The Lapsing engaged segment has 33,113 profiles — nearly half of your entire sendable list. But three potential winback flows — Subscriber Churn Winback, Relo Winback, and Activate One-Time Recent Buyers (the last is live but only reached 136 recipients in 180 days) — are either in draft or essentially idle.

For a subscription-first candle brand, winback is the #2 highest-leverage flow after welcome. A sub who skipped a quarter isn't gone — they're waiting for the right prompt. And a one-time buyer who's 90+ days out is 5× more likely to convert to a subscriber than a cold lead.

What to build:

  • Subscription Winback (3 emails over 14 days) for churned Smartrr subscribers: Email 1 — "Your seasonal scent just landed", Email 2 — "Here's what you missed" (shows the last box), Email 3 — "Rebuild your ritual" with 20% reactivation offer.
  • One-Time Buyer Winback (3 emails over 30 days) triggered at 90 days post-purchase for non-subscribers: Email 1 — scent check-in, Email 2 — "Try the box" (seasonal box subscription pitch with 45% first-box), Email 3 — "Switch to refills" with refill stock update.
  • Both flows should suppress from campaign sends during their active window — nothing kills a winback like being blasted with a generic promo email 2 days before the winback's discount email lands.
Revenue impact: If 3% of the 33k lapsing segment reconverts via winback (benchmark for food/bev-style subscription brands), that's ~990 orders × $80 AOV = +$79K annualised.
5. Post-purchase + cross-sell flows are stuck in draft
High Effort: Medium

You have three post-purchase-related draft flows that never shipped: Post Purchase – New & Returning – Sep 2025 (already generated $2,348 in whatever preview state it's in), Relo Cross-sell, and Cross sell/Up sell. The only live post-purchase automation is the Junip review request flow.

For a subscription brand where the natural customer journey is Buy a Forever Candle → Refill every season → Cross-sell into a second vessel / Ritual Set, missing this stage is money. The Reorder flow exists and works ($0.29 RPR) but it's treating everyone as a single-product buyer.

What to ship:

  • Activate Post Purchase – New & Returning – Sep 2025 — run the content against current brand tone, then enable. It's already drafted.
  • Build a Subscription Upsell flow for one-time Forever Candle buyers (Relo already has subscription conversion prediction data flowing in — metric Upgrade to subscription prediction by Blueprint is active). Trigger: 14 days after first purchase, if no subscription started. Pitch: "You just joined — let's make this a ritual" with the 45% first-box discount.
  • Add a Cross-sell Ritual Set flow for Forever Candle + 2 refills purchasers at 60 days post-purchase: Email 1 — "You're ready for the full ritual", Email 2 — social proof + UGC.
Revenue impact: If 5% of 16,424 "one-time customers" convert to subscription via the upsell flow (conservative — Smartrr's Blueprint prediction should boost this), that's ~820 conversions × avg subscription LTV contribution ~$60 email-attributable = +$49K annualised.
6. Zero-party data from quiz is collected but under-used
Medium Effort: Quick Win

The Candle Person Type quiz is generating exactly the signal every candle brand wishes they had: Calm (3,134 profiles), Comfort (2,598), Clarity (~2,000 est). Yet the three Candle People flows that consume this signal each triggered just 15–61 recipients over 180 days — effectively inactive.

This data should be powering conditional content blocks in every flow, not just dedicated welcome variants. A "Comfort" type gets different hero product recommendations in their winback email than a "Clarity" type. Klaviyo's native conditional template blocks handle this without any code.

What to do:

  • Audit the trigger of the three Candle People flows — they should be firing on every quiz completion, not just a subset. Confirm the list add is happening reliably.
  • Use {{ person.candle_type }} (or the actual property name) in conditional blocks across Welcome, Browse Abandonment, Reorder, and Winback flows — different hero product per type.
  • Add a subject-line personalisation token: "Sarah, your Calm scent just came back" vs "Sarah, your Clarity scent just came back" — same email, 2× subject-line relevance.
Revenue impact: Conservative 8–12% lift in click-through across personalised sections. On current campaign volume that's +$12–18K annualised, plus higher subscription conversion on welcome (harder to quantify but real).

2. 56% open rate, 0.46% click rate — hooks and CTAs aren't converting interest into action
High Effort: Medium

Opens are exceptional. Clicks are critical. That gap tells you the subject lines and preview text are doing their job of getting the email opened — but then the email content isn't moving people to click. Look at the worst performers:

  • Thanksgiving Recipe: 64.9% open, 0.11% click — great hook, content wasn't commerce-linked
  • Gift Cards: 56.5% open, 0.09% click, $0.017 RPR — 31k recipients saw it, 28 bought
  • Free expedited shipping: 49.2% open, 0.12% click — shipping alone isn't a reason-to-click

Compare to the winners:

  • XL Forever Candle – Oud & Peach: product launch, $0.366 RPR, 2.60% click — 5.6× account avg RPR
  • Persuadable Gifters: segmented send to a smart audience, $0.344 RPR, 1.68% click — segmentation beats blast
  • Black Week Sale Kick Off: $0.205 RPR at 40k recipients — the tentpole promo with clear urgency

The pattern: product launches and clearly-targeted sends with a single CTA outperform content emails with multiple links. Content emails (Notes from the North, recipe content, scent layering) are beautifully written but don't give the reader a clear next step.

What to fix:

  • Every editorial email needs a commerce hook in the bottom third — "Read more" isn't a CTA, "Shop the Forest Bathing bundle" is.
  • Start a formal A/B testing cadence — currently zero systematic testing is visible. Test subject lines on every campaign (Klaviyo native, 10–20% holdout).
  • Tighten subject-line format to 3–7 words with one emoji max. Current longer subject lines are hurting mobile rendering.
  • Kill the "Gift Cards" campaign format or heavily segment it — gift cards as a promotional standalone to 31k profiles performs worse than almost any other campaign type.
Revenue impact: Even a 50% lift in click rate (0.46% → 0.70%) at constant conversion lift rate adds +$50–70K annualised.
3. Same-day double sends are spiking unsubscribes
High Effort: Quick Win

In the 180-day window there are 13 same-day double-send occurrences plus 4 triple+ send days. And the unsubscribe pattern is consistent: the highest unsub event in the entire window was Happy New Year (196 unsubs / 0.639% rate) — sent on a day with another send.

Cyber Monday – Night – Text Only drove 171 unsubs at 0.423% — also same-day as the Cyber Monday main send.

This is a frequency-fatigue pattern. Subscribers accept 1 email on a given day; a second within hours feels spammy and triggers unsubs.

What to fix:

  • Introduce a hard frequency cap: max 1 campaign per day per subscriber. Use Klaviyo's built-in "received email in last 24 hours" exclusion on every send.
  • For tentpole events (BFCM, Spring Box launch), use layered segmentation instead of same-day doubles — morning send to engaged tier, afternoon send to broader tier, so no single person gets two emails.
  • The "Text Only" follow-up format is smart but should target non-openers of the main send, not the full list.
Revenue impact: Preserves ~2,000 subscribers/year who currently unsubscribe after same-day doubles. At conservative $50 LTV, +$100K over 12 months preserved subscriber value.
4. Content mix is genuinely good — but missing a few natural wins
Medium Effort: Medium

Rare to say this — your content mix is better than most audited accounts. Only 18.6% of campaigns are pure promo/sale, and you have strong editorial pillars (Notes from the North, Fika Time, scent-layering guides, Behind the scent series). That's the right instinct for a premium candle brand where the story sells the product.

What's missing for this brand specifically:

  • Scent & Stockholm origin stories. You sell Scandinavian sensibility. A "Meet the makers in Portugal" or "Why Stockholm winters shape our blends" campaign, done with photography and voice, would outperform any sale email for click-through.
  • Seasonal ritual emails. "The April morning ritual" / "Your Sunday reset" — product tied to moment of use, not just product spec.
  • Subscriber-exclusive drops. A Seasonal Box subscriber should get a campaign their one-time peers never see — a preview scent, an early refill, a Stockholm-shipped postcard. This drives subscription signups via FOMO when you do do a public promo.
  • Customer spotlights & UGC roundups. The reviews on your PDPs ("elegant design", "pleasant experience") are warm but generic — a monthly "Customers' favourite corner" campaign with photos of candles in real homes gives product social proof at scale.

Also kill: Shipping-based promos as standalone campaigns (Free expedited shipping, Last call for standard shipping) — these are great as a banner inside a product campaign, not as a whole send.

Revenue impact: 2 additional brand-story campaigns per month at your content-email RPR ($0.052) across 26k recipients = +$16K annualised, plus higher subscription signup through FOMO.
