We rebuilt the flow to discount only to new customers and hold off discounting repeats — to protect your L1 sale periods.

The previous flow handed every abandoner a flat 25% off from the second email onwards. The rebuild trades differently. New customers now move through a laddered offer — 20% first, bumped to 25% only if they don't convert — while repeat customers receive no discount at all.

The reasoning is calendar discipline. Repeat customers already get 25% off during your L1 campaign sales. Building that same 25% into an always-on flow would slowly train them to abandon at checkout and wait for the email, rather than buying during the sale — quietly eroding the very campaigns the flow is meant to support. So we discount hard to win new customers, and keep repeat discounting where it earns its margin: inside your L1 calendar.

With no discount in front of them, repeat-customer conversion drops to 3.3%, from 8.73% on the prior flow. That fall is the discount doing its work — the old flat 25% was tipping a large share of repeats who, in many cases, would have bought during an L1 sale anyway. Removing it costs conversions now, but stops the always-on flow from cannibalising those campaigns.
