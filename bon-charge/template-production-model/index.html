The boundary

Wellness variants only live in Welcome 1, 2 and 3.

Wellness variants are not used across the whole welcome flow. They live in the first three emails, where personalising against the customer's stated wellness goal lifts conversion. The remaining three welcome emails are a single email each, sent to every user regardless of wellness goal.

Every flow after welcome

Abandoned cart and checkout: only 2 variants each.

Wellness-goal variants are welcome-flow specific. Every flow we build from here, starting with abandoned cart and checkout abandonment, uses a much simpler structure: one bespoke template per email, with just two variants. New customer vs. retaining customer.

  • Retaining customer (has purchased)
  • One copy edit updates instantly across both
On the edit-volume concern

Even with the templating proven, it's fair to still worry about volume.

Showing that variants are just the same template with products swapped doesn't make the email count disappear on paper. So here is how it breaks down, and where the edit surface actually sits.

Wellness variants live in Welcome 1, 2 and 3 only.

Three emails, four wellness variants each. That is the entire scope of the wellness-variant approach. They exist because the customer hands us their wellness goal at signup, and that single piece of zero-party data makes personalising the first three emails worth the effort.

Welcome 4, 5 and 6 are a single email each. No variants.

One email goes to every customer regardless of wellness goal, the same way the rest of the welcome series lands in every inbox.

Every flow after welcome uses just two variants per template: new vs. retaining customer.

Abandoned cart and abandoned checkout first. No wellness splits, no region duplicates. One copy update lands on both variants at once.

Tethrd's design team makes the change across the relevant variants and re-uploads to Klaviyo.

That is our production model on our side, not work that lands with the BON CHARGE team. You raise the change, we apply it across every variant, every region, every flow.

Bottom line

The build covers the bespoke templates, once. Variants aren't new template builds. They're the same templates with products swapped and copy tightened. The wellness-variant approach exists in Welcome 1, 2 and 3 only, where zero-party signup data justifies it. Welcome 4, 5 and 6 are single emails. Abandoned cart and checkout use a simple new-vs-retaining two-variant split where one edit updates both sides instantly. Tethrd handles the edits. That's not work that lands with the BON CHARGE team.
