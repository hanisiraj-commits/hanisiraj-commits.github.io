Variants aren't
new templates.

Welcome Email 1 is one bespoke template. We call it Email 1.1, the Skin version. Emails 1.2, 1.3 and 1.6 (Hair, Recovery and Sleep) are variants of that same template, with products swapped and copy tightened to match the wellness goal the customer told us at signup. Same structure end to end. Same sections in the same order. Different products inside. That's a variant, not another template.

Template vs. variant

Two distinct things. Treating them as the same is what's making one bespoke build look like many.

Abandoned cart and checkout flows: only 2 variants each

Wellness-goal variants are welcome-flow specific. Every flow we build from here, starting with abandoned cart and checkout abandonment, uses a much simpler structure: one bespoke template per email, with just two variants. New customer vs. retaining customer.

On the edit-volume concern
Even with the templating proven, it's fair to still worry about volume. Here's why that volume is much smaller than it looks, and why Tethrd carries it.

Showing that variants are just the same template with products swapped doesn't make the email count disappear on paper. So here is how it breaks down, and where the edit surface actually sits.

Wellness variants live in Welcome 1, 2 and 3 only. Three emails, four wellness variants each. That is the entire scope of the wellness-variant approach. They exist because the customer hands us their wellness goal at signup, and that single piece of zero-party data makes personalising the first three emails worth the effort.

Welcome 4, 5 and 6 are a single email each. No variants. One email goes to every customer regardless of wellness goal, the same way the rest of the welcome series lands in every inbox.

Every flow after welcome (abandoned cart and abandoned checkout first) uses just two variants per template: new customer vs. retaining customer. No wellness splits, no region duplicates. One copy update lands on both variants at once.

And on the edits themselves: Tethrd's design team makes the change across the relevant variants and re-uploads to Klaviyo. That is our production model on our side, not work that lands with the BON CHARGE team. You raise the change, we apply it across every variant, every region, every flow.

The build covers the bespoke templates, once. Variants aren't new template builds. They're the same templates with products swapped and copy tightened. The wellness-variant approach exists in Welcome 1, 2 and 3 only, where zero-party signup data justifies it. Welcome 4, 5 and 6 are single emails. That's not work that lands with the BON CHARGE team.
