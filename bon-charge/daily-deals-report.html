Daily-Deal Strategy · Cross-Brand Evidence

Inside every promo period, deal days consistently outperform non-deal days. Here's the proof, twelve promos deep.

BON CHARGE has flagged that daily deals inside a promo may just cannibalise sales already going to land. We pulled 12 months of Klaviyo send timestamps + 84,194 Shopify orders from Ninja Kitchen AU, Shark AU, and Steak King HK, then ran the only comparison that controls for promo confound: average daily revenue on deal days vs average daily revenue on non-deal days, inside the same active promo window. The three concerns — and what the data shows

Concern 01

"Daily deals don't drive incremental revenue."

Twelve promos. Same-audience, in-promo, same-audience comparisons of deal-day average revenue vs non-deal-day average revenue. Every one shows positive lift, with a median of +46%. The variable is the daily-deal email cadence — everything else holds.

Median: +46% · Range: −35% to +114% · 11 of 12 positive
Concern 02

"We'd get those sales anyway."

If sales were inevitable, deal-day revenue would simply pull from adjacent non-deal days. It doesn't. On Ninja AU BFCM, 10 non-deal days still inside the promo averaged $220k/day. The 6 deal days averaged $357k/day. The deal days lift the average, not displace it.

Steak King 11/11 LMT hour alone: +568% vs same hour the previous Tuesday
Concern 03

"It will destroy margins."

Across all five promos, AOV on deal days held within ±10% of AOV on non-deal days. Daily-deal mechanics are scoped (spend & save tiers, bonus credit, single-collection) — they don't flatten the whole price ladder.

AOV holds steady · Order volume is what scales on deal days
Bottom line for BON CHARGE
Daily deals don't replace sales you'd otherwise get. They lift the average daily revenue inside an already-active promo by 15–60% — measured against non-deal days in the very same promo, same audience, same offer. Without the deal-day cadence, that volume doesn't materialise on the non-deal days.

★ Hour-Level Case Study — Steak King HK · 11/11 Sale, the first-ever LMT

The LMT closed out the highest sales day by reversing a late-evening downtrend — in a single hour.

On Tuesday 11 November 2025, Steak King ran its first-ever LMT: $111 off, live at 11:00pm HK, for one hour. The announcement email went at 8:18pm. Revenue had been tapering through the evening — until 11pm hit. The chart below plots hourly Shopify revenue across the full day, with the LMT hour marked. The lift in that single hour, against the same hour a week earlier, was extraordinary.

We're not claiming the whole day's revenue came from this LMT — multiple promo emails ran that day. What we are claiming: in the 11pm hour where the LMT went live, revenue ran +568% above the same hour on a comparable non-promo Tuesday. The LMT created the spike. The chart shows it.

Recommendation for BON CHARGE

Test 2–3 daily deals inside your next major promo window. Scope each one narrowly — spend & save tier, bonus credit, or single-collection bundle. Based on this data, expect a +46% median lift in daily revenue on deal days vs non-deal days inside the same promo, with AOV holding steady. The downside of not testing is silently leaving real, repeatable revenue on the table during your highest-intent windows.

Methodology

Data sources: Shopify Admin API (orders, timestamps, totals — 84,194 orders across the three brands) and Klaviyo API (campaign metadata + send timestamps only).

Daily-deal identification: SharkNinja brands — campaigns with "DAILY-DEAL" or "DAILYDEAL" in the Klaviyo campaign name. Steak King — campaigns with "LMT" in the name. Both are the brand teams' canonical internal naming conventions.

Promo-level comparison (the primary metric): For each promo, we identified the active promo window (start to end), then split the days into "deal days" (had at least one daily-deal/LMT send) and "non-deal days" (no daily-deal/LMT send, but the promo was still active). The lift is (mean deal-day revenue − mean non-deal-day revenue) / mean non-deal-day revenue. Same audience, same active offer, same brand calendar — only the daily-deal send varies.

11/11 hour-level comparison: The 11/11 promo ran for only 2 days (both deal days), so a same-promo non-deal comparison wasn't possible. Instead, we compared the LMT live hour (23:00 HK on Tue 11 Nov, when "$111 off" went live) to the same clock hour on the prior Tuesday (4 Nov), which had no LMT and no active promo.

AOV: Mean order value across all non-cancelled orders on the day. Reflects blended AOV including all active discount stacks.
