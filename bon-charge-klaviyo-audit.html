COMPLIANCE GAPS

Two compliance gaps to fix this week

Bon Charge runs one Klaviyo account for US, UK, and Australia. Two specific gaps create real legal exposure — both are quick fixes worth making before the next campaign sends.

Critical — Legal Risk Quick Win Effort: 1 hour
1. Footer is missing a visible postal address

Inspecting campaign and flow templates, there's no visible text postal address in the footer of Bon Charge's emails. CAN-SPAM (US), CASL (Canada), Spam Act 2003 (Australia), and GDPR/PECR (UK/EU) all require a clearly displayed physical postal address of the sender on every commercial email. Right now, recipients can't easily see one.

Any single recipient could file a complaint with their regulator and the path to a settlement starts. CAN-SPAM penalties alone are up to $51,744 per violation; CASL is up to CAD $10M per violation. Regulators pursue these specifically because they're easy wins.

Fix this week:

  • Edit the master email footer template. Add a visible text block with: company legal name (BLUBlox Pty Ltd t/a Bon Charge), full street address, and the unsubscribe link.
  • Use country-conditional rendering (you already have {% if person.location.country %} blocks elsewhere) to show a US-relevant address for US recipients if applicable, and the AU registered office for everyone else.
  • Apply across all live flow templates and the campaign master template.
Cost of inaction: $50K-$10M+ in regulatory exposure. Cost of fix: 1 hour.
High Priority — GDPR Effort: Medium
2. UK / EU sending isn't separated from implicit-consent regions

The 25,361-profile UK Segment exists, but campaign audiences mostly send via the broader "Engaged 0-180D" or "10-day engaged" segments — which don't filter for explicit GDPR-style consent. UK and EU subscribers require active opt-in (not implied or pre-ticked). Profiles added without explicit consent shouldn't be receiving marketing email at all.

What needs to change:

  • Geo-targeted popups: The main popup needs a UK/EU variant with explicit consent language ("I consent to receive marketing emails from Bon Charge"), no pre-ticked checkboxes, and a clear opt-out path.
  • UK-specific sending segment: Build "UK + Confirmed Opt-In" segment definition. Use this as the inclusion for any campaign that includes UK recipients. Exclude implicit-consent profiles entirely.
  • Flow cloning for UK: The Welcome Series, Browse Abandonment, and Cart Abandonment flows currently fire on any profile regardless of region. Clone them and add region-conditional logic — UK profiles only enter if they have explicit consent on file.
  • Checkout consent capture: Verify the Shopify checkout marketing consent checkbox shows for UK/EU customers and isn't pre-ticked.
Cost of inaction: GDPR fines up to 4% of global turnover or €20M, whichever is higher.

1. Welcome Series is 50-60% of flow revenue from 3 emails — extend with zero-party data

The Welcome Series produces $1.087M / 180 days at $10.81 RPR — exceptional. But it's only 3 emails over 2 days, with no personalisation. For a brand selling $200-$3,349 wellness devices across 6 categories with high educational complexity, that's leaving the biggest revenue lever untouched.

The right approach is paired: extend the series AND capture zero-party data at signup so the extension is personalised, not generic. Without data the extra emails are just more volume; with data they're tailored education that pays off.

Step 1 — capture in the popup:

  • Add a single question after email submission: "What's your #1 health goal?"
  • Options: Better Sleep / Faster Recovery / Skin & Anti-Aging / EMF Protection / Overall Wellness
  • Store as profile property. Drives every downstream personalisation.

Step 2 — rebuild Welcome as 5 emails over 7 days, conditional on health goal:

  • Email 1 (immediate): Welcome + 15% offer + brand authority hero (Scientific Advisory Board, 400K customers, Fulham FC). Common to all paths.
  • Email 2 (24hr): "Here's why [Sleep / Recovery / Skin / EMF] matters" — educational deep-dive matched to their answer. Different copy per path.
  • Email 3 (Day 3): Hero product for their goal (Sleep → Sleep Mask + Blackout Stack; Recovery → Red Light + PEMF; Skin → Face Mask). Plain-text founder/scientist email.
  • Email 4 (Day 5): Social proof — review montage + athlete UGC for their category.
  • Email 5 (Day 7): Final discount reminder + bundle pivot if they didn't convert on Email 3.

Personalised welcome flows in audited accounts produce 30-50% more revenue than generic ones, and unsub rates drop because the content matches the subscriber's stated interest.

Revenue at stake: +$400K-$700K / yr
Critical Effort: Medium Pillar: Flow Triggers
2. Switch the Add-to-Cart trigger from API integration to Shopify integration

Bon Charge currently has the live Abandoned Cart flow triggered off the API integration "Added to Cart" metric (Klaviyo's older event source — SGN3DX). Klaviyo deprecated this approach ~2 years ago in favour of the Shopify integration "Added to Cart" metric (YqTY7r). Both metrics are firing in the account today:

  • API integration ATC: 191,897 events / 180d (currently the trigger)
  • Shopify integration ATC: 130,214 events / 180d (currently unused for the flow)

Across every client we've migrated, the Shopify-integration ATC metric converts materially better than the API-tracked one. Reason: it's more accurate. The API metric fires on onsite cart actions whether or not the cart actually persists at the Shopify level, leading to "false trigger" recipients (people who clicked but didn't actually have a session that became a cart). The Shopify webhook fires only on real cart-update events tied to a session.

What to do:

  • Clone the current live Abandoned Cart flow. Set the new clone's trigger to the Shopify integration "Added to Cart" metric.
  • Add a "Has been in this flow recently" filter and a profile-level dedup check on both flows so a single user doesn't get hit twice (once from API, once from Shopify).
  • Activate the Shopify-trigger flow as the primary; keep the API-trigger flow live with the dedup filter as a fallback.
  • Monitor for 14-30 days, compare RPR + unsub rate. Once Shopify trigger is proven to outperform, archive the API-trigger flow.
Revenue at stake: +$50K-$150K / yr (cleaner triggers + better RPR)

3. Post-Purchase is one thank-you + 20% off — no education, no nurture, no cross-sell

The Post-Purchase Thank You Series (UjZGex) does $22K / 180d at $1.73 RPR. Right now it's essentially: thank you + 20% off your next order, sent immediately after purchase. There's no dedicated post-purchase educational + nurture + cross-sell architecture. The core problem: we're trying to sell the next thing before they've even unboxed the first one.

For a brand selling $200-$3,349 devices with real educational complexity (red light protocols, PEMF settings, EMF stacking strategies), post-purchase should first ensure customers get more use out of the product they just bought. Only after they've activated and are satisfied with the product should we start nurturing them toward cross-sell and upsell.

Rebuild as 3-stage NPS-driven sequence:

  1. Stage 1 (Day 0-14) — Activation. Order confirmation, "what to expect", how-to-use guides matched to product purchased, founder/scientist context on protocol, review request at Day 12. Goal is product activation, not selling.
  2. Stage 2 (Day 14-X, X = brand-specific average) — Satisfied → cross-sell. Don't pick an arbitrary day. Pull the actual data: what's the average days-to-second-purchase for repeat customers? That number is your X. Trigger Stage 2 emails 7 days before X and at X. Cross-sell into adjacent categories (the Holistic Beauty Customer Journey pattern already exists for Face Mask and Neck/Chest Mask buyers — extend to all 6 categories).
  3. Stage 3 (Day 60+) — VIP gateway. Customers who hit 2+ orders auto-enter VIP path. Currently the VIP Early Access flow sits in DRAFT — activate it with quarterly exclusivity content (early access, member-only bundles, podcast access).

On the existing product-specific upgrade flows: The Holistic Beauty Neck & Chest Mask flow uses a generic 180-day delay. Replace with data-driven personalisation — pull the actual days-to-upgrade distribution from order data, set delays accordingly. Same logic for Face Mask → Wand → Blanket upgrade paths.

Revenue at stake: +$300K-$500K / yr (cross-sell + repeat purchase rate lift)

Campaign Improvement Areas

Critical Effort: Quick Pillar: Frequency / Cadence
1. Drop campaign frequency to 2-4 / week and follow the engagement-tier cadence

April hit 45 sends in 30 days (1.5/day). RPR has fallen 66% since November ($0.156 → $0.053). Subscribers are fatiguing — and the data shows it cleanly: even with the engaged-only audience, RPR is below benchmark, and unsub rate hit 0.33% in Jan.

The fix isn't just "send less" — it's send less, to wider audiences, on a tier-cadence cycle:

  • 30D Engaged (~122K): Max every alternate day. The most active core. Daily content and sale stages.
  • 60D Engaged (~144K): 2-3 sends / week. Slightly lower-frequency content + sale touches.
  • 90D Engaged (~161K): 1-2 sends / week. Weekly digest, monthly product education, sale tease.
  • 180D Engaged (~212K): 1 send / week max. Monthly polished content piece + sale.
  • 365D Engaged (build this segment): 1 send / month. Re-engagement / sunset prelude.
  • Whole list: 1 send / month MAX, reserved exclusively for major sales (BFCM, Recharge, Memorial, Holiday) and new-product launch announcements.

Important: even 180D engaged subscribers show 50%+ open rates on the rare campaigns they receive. That segment is healthy — it's just being under-used. Cycling content through 30 / 60 / 90 / 180 / 365 / whole-list buckets gives the program more reach with less frequency-per-subscriber. Net effect: RPR recovers, unsubs drop, list health holds.

Conservative target: drop total send volume 30% (from 6.8/wk to ~4.5/wk avg), widen audience by 60%, recover 50% of lost RPR.

Revenue at stake: +$500K-$900K / yr
High Priority Effort: Medium Pillar: Content Mix
2. Build a monthly content calendar where educational content sets up product sales

Current mix: 86% revenue from promotional pushes, 10% from educational, the rest is brand/partnership. The data tells us that educational has higher engagement (72% open rate vs 50% promo), but doesn't convert because it sits unconnected from the sales arc.

Restructure the calendar so each month has a coherent product-led story:

  • Each month picks one hero product or category. e.g. May = Sleep Mask + Blackout. July = PEMF / Sauna. October = Red Light Therapy.
  • Educational content (40% of monthly sends) early and mid-month builds the case for that hero — the science, athlete UGC, customer stories, comparison guides, doctor features. These are tied to the hero product, not random topics.
  • Product-specific sale (30% of monthly sends) closes the month — a 3-5 day sale on the hero category. The educational content of the prior weeks has done the heavy lift of skepticism elimination, so the sale converts harder.
  • Major tentpole sales (15%) — 4-6 events / yr (BFCM, Recharge, Memorial, Christmas etc.). Whole-list eligible. The frequency-cap exception window.
  • Pure brand authority (15%) — podcast features, athlete partnerships, newsletter pieces. Sent to engaged-only segments. These don't need to convert in-window; they build long-term trust.

Educational content can also reach a wider audience tier than promotional sends — content sends cause less unsub harm at 60D+ engaged audiences than discount pushes do, so they're the right vehicle to gently re-engage less-active subscribers.

Revenue at stake: +$300K-$500K / yr
High Priority Effort: Quick Pillar: VIP Targeting
3. Treat VIPs as VIPs — dedicated campaigns + member-only access

VIP campaigns convert at $0.90 RPR — 10-15x broader campaign rates. But the VIP audience (14,467 profiles) only receives 1-2 dedicated campaigns / month. The rest of the time they're treated identically to the broad list.

For a brand actively trying to grow VIP membership, this is the wrong direction. Every regular campaign that goes to a VIP subscriber is a missed signal — they're paying for VIP status, they should feel it.

Build a VIP campaign rhythm:

  • 1-2 VIP-only campaigns per week: Special discounts, member pricing on bundles, free gifts with purchase, podcast access, Q&A invites.
  • Insider content access: Exclusive podcast episodes, founder/scientist Q&A recordings, early product previews. Make membership feel like a club.
  • VIP-first launches: New product drops go to VIPs 24-48 hours before general list. Drives both retention and acquisition (people join VIP to get access).
