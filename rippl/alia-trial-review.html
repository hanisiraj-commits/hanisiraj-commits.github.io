Section 1

The benchmark

Alia ran 9 June to 20 July but was only live for 33 of those days, since we took it down twice for site changes. Comparing 33 live days against a full 42-day benchmark would flatter Klaviyo, so the benchmark is the 33 trading days immediately before Alia went live, 7 May to 8 June. Same length, back to back, no overlap.

Traffic was not identical across the two windows. The store did 16,887 sessions in the benchmark period against 15,607 on Alia's live days, so Alia was working with 8% less traffic. Both platforms showed the pop-up to a near-identical share of that traffic (60.4% of sessions for Klaviyo, 61.9% for Alia), which makes the rate comparison a fair one. Where it matters, the table below normalises per 1,000 sessions so the traffic gap doesn't distort the read.

On revenue we're not taking Alia's reporting at face value. Alia's dashboard claims $66,851 of attributed sales, but that counts pre-discount order values and credits every sign-up it recorded, including ones that never reached our list. Every revenue figure here is measured in Klaviyo, off the Subscribed to Email Marketing event and Shopify's Placed Order metric, applied identically to both platforms. Sign-ups from the Shopify checkout and the EOFY landing page are excluded from both, since neither pop-up produced them. Pop-up views and submit rates come from each platform's own reporting, because neither tool can see the other's impressions. Sessions come from Shopify. Figures are AUD.

Section 3

What this means

Alia converts more of the same traffic and brings in better buyers. Both halves of that matter, and the second one matters more.

On capture, Alia lifts the submit rate from 5.41% to 6.98%. Normalised for the traffic difference it produced 43.2 sign-ups per 1,000 sessions against Klaviyo's 32.6, a 32% improvement. That gain comes from things a table can't show: multi-step flows, better targeting, and design and layout control that Klaviyo's native pop-up doesn't give us.

The more valuable finding is what those sign-ups are worth. Alia's subscribers convert to an order at nearly double the rate and are worth 75% more each. Over 33 days that's $10,319 of additional revenue on 8% less traffic. At roughly $200 USD a month, Alia has returned more than thirty times its cost.

One thing to fix. Of the 674 email sign-ups Alia recorded, 418 reached Klaviyo, against 518 of 551 for the Klaviyo pop-up. Some of that gap is normal deduplication, but not all of it, and an address that doesn't arrive is one we can't market to. I'm raising it with Alia. It's the biggest lever available, because the revenue above was produced by only 62% of what Alia actually captured.
