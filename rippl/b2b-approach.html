Where B2B sits today

Quick read on the current state before the rest of this makes sense. Four things matter.

01
The buyer is a Marketing Manager
Persona
Bade's deck names the persona. The person buying Custom Branded at L'Oréal, Mercedes, or New Balance is a marketing manager. Tone, content, and offers all have to land for that role.
02
95% visit the site first
Funnel
The Pipedrive funnel was built laser-tight on purpose. Pricing, timelines, and social proof all upfront so only high-intent people submit. That's why inquiry-to-win sits at ~80%. It also means every lower-intent visitor leaves no trace.
03
Two model shifts inside 60 days
Roadmap
Self-customiser drops MOQ from 240 to 120 (live in ~6 weeks). End of July the business switches to on-demand. Both mean reorder cadence becomes per-account, not per-calendar, and the email program has to follow.
04
Sample pack works. Reorder leaks.
Gap
$20 sample pack does its job. What's missing is everything after the first custom order. No post-purchase flow, no personalised reorder reminder, no recovery on dormant accounts. Customers reorder a week after an EDM the AM picks up, and Klaviyo never sees it.

Where the funnel breaks

Three breaks, in priority order. Fixing them in sequence is what unlocks the rest of the program.

01
Lower-intent traffic walks
Top of funnel
The Custom Branded page and vessel pages get visible traffic. None of it is captured before the high-intent form. The form was designed for high intent only, which made sense before there was a lifecycle to nurture into. There is one now.
02
Pipedrive data stops at Pipedrive
Data sync
Outfunnel syncs the email. The rest of the form (vessel, quantity, deadline, role, design context) never reaches Klaviyo. Every B2B email is generic because of it.
03
Reorders happen off-platform
Reorder
May EDM with timelines drove one direct response. The AMs picked up several more reorders 7–10 days later, untagged. People are reading and acting. Klaviyo just doesn't see it, and the next send can't learn from it.

The approach

Five pillars, sequenced over 90 days. Pillars 1 and 2 unlock the rest.

01
ALIA pop-up on the Custom Branded funnel
Capture
ALIA pop-up on the Custom Branded page and each vessel page, capturing email from the cold tier the existing form is too heavy to catch. Incentive to be confirmed. We'll trial a few before locking one.
02
Pipedrive → Klaviyo data sync
Foundation
Every form field becomes a Klaviyo property. A B2B Order Placed event fires on the Pipedrive Won stage so we can read conversion rate directly. Full spec on its own page for Moti, linked below.
03
One B2B welcome flow
Activation
One welcome flow for B2B subscribers. We'll work out the structure (single linear vs split by source) at implementation time, once we see how the ALIA captures, sample-pack buyers, and custom-form submitters actually distribute.
04
Personalised reorder flow
Retention
Triggered when a Pipedrive deal moves to Won. Dynamic wait tied to each account's reorder cycle. The email pre-fills their last order with a one-click reorder link. Fixes the leakage you flagged twice on the call.
05
Dormant recovery + objection survey
Recovery
When a Pipedrive deal goes Lost or Dormant, Klaviyo fires a short founder-led survey ("what stopped you?") plus a 30-day soft re-engagement track. Recovers some, feeds product input back to you.
