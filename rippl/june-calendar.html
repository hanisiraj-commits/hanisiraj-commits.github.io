Campaign by campaign

12 sends. What each is, when it goes.

Week 1 — Beauty Chef VIP activation

1 – 7 Jun · 1 send

One send to start the month. The Beauty Chef opt-in goes early so we have nine days for confirmations to come in before EOFY launches.

01
Beauty Chef list — VIP opt-in for EOFY early access
Sat 6 Jun
List activation · EOFY-anchored
First email to the ~4,500 leads from February's Beauty Chef giveaway. We ask them to confirm they want to hear from Rippl, framed as joining a VIP list with early access to the EOFY drop ($225 off Pure plus the free glass bottle). Cleans the list and teases the sale in the one send.

Week 2 — Re-warm + two-step EOFY tease

8 – 14 Jun · 4 sends

World Oceans Day re-introduces the brand on Monday. B2B reorder sits alongside Tuesday (different audience). Then nothing until Friday, when the first EOFY teaser goes. Second teaser Sunday. Launch Monday.

02
World Oceans Day — CleanHub partnership EDM
Mon 8 Jun
Brand · Purpose-led
Using Charlotte's CleanHub draft plus the animated GIF she sent through. Leads with the 859kg plastic-recovered stat. No discount, no sale push — purely a brand send, which is what we want for the first proper re-warm after the domain hold. 8 June is also King's Birthday, so we'll catch strong inbox attention on a usually quiet Monday.
03
B2B reorder reminder
Tue 9 Jun
B2B · Corporate accounts
A reminder to existing corporate customers to put their reorder in before the production cut-off. Leads with the deadline and what they miss if they wait. Main CTA is to their account manager, secondary is the self-serve inquiry form.
04
EOFY Teaser 1 — save the date
Fri 12 Jun
EOFY · Soft teaser
Soft save-the-date. "Monday 15 June. Something for your water." No mention of the discount or the bottle yet. Three days out, just enough to plant the seed without giving away the reveal.
05
EOFY Teaser 2 — the reveal
Sun 14 Jun
EOFY · Hard teaser
Day-before reveal. "Tomorrow: $225 off Rippl Pure plus a free glass bottle with every purchase." Full offer on the table this time. CTA goes to the landing page. Sunday evening send so it's the first thing they see Monday.

Week 3 — EOFY launches

15 – 21 Jun · 3 sends

Sale opens Monday. Launch goes out, then a three-day pause, then a two-day pause. Three sends across the week.

06
EOFY LAUNCH — $225 off Pure + free glass bottle
Mon 15 Jun
EOFY · Launch
The launch email. $225 off Pure with a free glass bottle on every order. Hero image is Pure and the bottle together. Subject line leads with the offer. 6pm resend to non-openers with a different subject so we don't leave first-day revenue sitting in unopened inboxes.
07
Customer story — why Rippl Pure
Thu 18 Jun
EOFY · Social proof
One real customer. Their water concern, why they picked Rippl Pure over the alternatives, what's different now. Written as a story rather than a sales email. CTA back to the offer at the end.
08
Tax-time cost framing
Sat 20 Jun
EOFY · Cost angle
For the buyers who want to justify it on numbers. Frames Pure as a one-time tax-year spend that pays itself back against a year of bottled water. Plays into the EOFY headspace where everyone's already running annual maths in their head.

Week 4 — Mid-sale push

22 – 28 Jun · 3 sends

Glass bottle urgency Tuesday, B2B EOFY-budget Wednesday, weekend urgency Saturday. Spaced out so nothing lands back-to-back.

09
The glass bottle is going fast
Tue 23 Jun
EOFY · GWP urgency
Mid-sale send that puts the glass bottle in the centre. "The free glass bottle has [X] orders against it — it's becoming the thing people are claiming this drop for." Makes the bottle feel desirable rather than a throw-in, which gives the fence-sitters a reason to act without us touching the discount lever again.
10
B2B EOFY budget — lock orders before FY close
Wed 24 Jun
B2B · EOFY-budget play
B2B-only send. "Remaining FY25 marketing budget? Lock your custom-branded can order this week. Invoices issued by 30 June qualify for this financial year." The one time all year the use-it-or-lose-it budget conversation actually lands with corporate buyers.
11
Last weekend urgency
Sat 27 Jun
EOFY · Urgency
"Last weekend to lock the EOFY drop." Deadline-led copy, a short list of what's in (price, the bottle, delivery), one CTA. Goes to anyone who's opened an EOFY email and hasn't bought yet.

Week 5 — Close

29 – 30 Jun · 1 send

Single send. Final 24 hours. Maximum urgency.

12
Final 24 hours — close out
Tue 30 Jun
EOFY · Final
Hard close. "Last 24 hours. $225 off plus the free glass bottle. Both disappear at midnight." Subject line leads with the hours remaining. 5pm resend to non-openers with a different subject. Strip the body back to the offer, the deadline, the CTA.

What we need from you to lock the plan in

Three things.

01
EOFY landing page status
Is the early sign-up landing page (the one the Beauty Chef VIP opt-in and Teaser 1 redirect to) on track for 6 June? If it's at risk, both campaigns can point at the Pure product page instead and we'll drop the early-signup mechanic for this run.
02
B2B reorder cut-off date
What's the usual production and fulfilment cut-off date corporate customers need to reorder by? From what we've seen it looks like mid-month, but I'm not sure if that's a continuous monthly thing we can write the campaign against or a one-off this cycle. Need your read on it before we lock the send date.
03
One strong piece of social proof
For the customer story on 18 June, can you share one strong piece of social proof that's been performing for you? UGC is ideal if you've got it. Otherwise a customer we can profile, or something we can pull from existing reviews.
