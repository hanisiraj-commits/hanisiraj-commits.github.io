01Why we ran it

The store bets the month on ten days and two SKUs

This test was the cheapest way to find out whether a daily, non-hero deal can carry more of the ordinary days, without leaning harder on a tired list.

57% of a month lands on ten trading days. Our repeat buyers — three quarters of the revenue — order roughly once every five weeks, and they have been trained to hold that one purchase for Meat Emporium. So BAU days sag, and when the M9 Ribeye and Striploin sold out mid-sale in June, there was no second engine to catch the day. This test points a deal at the other twenty days, on cuts below the heroes, to see if it moves the number.

What we wanted it to prove

  • De-risk the top five. Sell the day without touching the M9 heroes.
  • Push product mix. Put secondary cuts in front of the whole list.
  • Front-load revenue onto BAU days to de-risk this month's Meat Emporium.
  • Pull frequency up: a daily reason to check the site (directional; three days can't prove this yet).

The honest caveat

This does lean us more promotional. Worth saying out loud. But with ~57% of revenue already coming from one monthly sale, we're promo-led today regardless. This spreads the promotion into a daily habit rather than one cliff a month, and keeps the deepest mechanics reserved for the sale.

02What ran, and how each day did

One deal, 24 hours, never a hero cut

Every deal followed the same rule: one product, live for 24 hours, never an M9 hero. We rotate the cut around the catalogue and build each offer past ~$700, so the customer adds the deal plus maybe one more item to clear the $1,000 free-shipping line. Under each deal: how the day traded against the average clean same-weekday BAU day, and how the featured cut itself sold against its 30-day norm.

The cut on the day: Black Angus M2-3 Rump Cap sold 14 units / $10,087. This cut was out of stock through most of June and only relisted on 13 July, so a 30-day average understates it. That's the expected trade on a deal day, and it tells us the lift is real order volume, not a discount inflating a few baskets. Every deal ran on a cut below the M9 heroes, so the heroes were never discounted and stayed reserved for the sale.
