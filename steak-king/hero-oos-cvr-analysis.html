For David · Stock-out impact

When our core cuts sell out,
store conversion follows.

We backfilled the last 90 days of normal trading and split them into days our core wagyu cuts were in stock vs out of stock. When a core cut is out, store-wide conversion dips ~7% and revenue per session drops ~19%. Sale periods are set aside, since a sale lifts conversion on its own.

3Revenue per session — in stock vs out of stock

Store revenue ÷ site sessions on normal trading days. This is where the stock-out bites hardest.

How to read it. A core cut recording zero sales on a normal day is our signal it was out of stock. Across the last 90 days there were 26 fully-stocked days and 12 stock-out days. On the stock-out days the whole store converted lower and earned less per visitor, because shoppers either leave or trade down when the premium cuts are gone. Sale days are excluded. Core cuts: M9+ Ribeye, M9+ Striploin, M4-5 Fillet.
