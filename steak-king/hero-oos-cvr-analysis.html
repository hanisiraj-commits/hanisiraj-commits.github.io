Why 90 days, not 365. These three cuts only became reliable daily sellers in early April. Before that they sold a few units a day, so a zero-sales day meant low demand, not a stock-out — and a full-year version wrongly flags ~70% of days as out of stock, which buries the real effect. The 90-day window is the honest read: it covers every genuine stock-out since these became daily heroes. The signal is sharpest in the last 30 days, where the M9+ Ribeye and Striploin were both out for long stretches.
