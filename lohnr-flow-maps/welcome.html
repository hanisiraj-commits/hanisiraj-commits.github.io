Creative asset brief

Per-image creative briefs for the client's creators, written the way we brief campaigns: each one reads as a full alt-text brief a photographer can shoot straight from. Product grids pull dynamically from the store feed. Heroes are full-bleed 600px, static, warm natural light, minimal and editorial.

Shoot · creators Dynamic feed · no shoot Client-supplied Source / UGC Design in-build
E1welcome / brand world
SHOOTHero: brand-world lifestyle (static)
A woman at home in a Lohnr set, caught mid-morning in soft natural light. She's doing something ordinary, coffee in hand, no rush. Full-bleed and static, warm and editorial. This is the world, not the product. Nothing in the frame is for sale yet, just a life worth getting dressed for.
SHOOTLifestyle detail
A close, unposed frame of the fabric as she moves, the drape of a sleeve or the way a hem falls when she shifts. It should let you feel the quality without a single spec written down.
DYNAMIC FEEDNew-arrivals 2x2 product block
Pulled live from the new-arrivals collection. Four current pieces, image and name, no prices. It updates itself as the collection changes, so the email never goes stale.
DESIGNTrust / USP strip
A quiet band under the fold: free shipping over $75, easy returns, trusted by 10,000+ happy customers. Small type with room to breathe, reassurance rather than a sales banner.
Brief for designer · Row 6RO team: Hero must read as brand-world, not a product or offer shot. No code anywhere in this email. Confirm the returns-window wording before it ships.
E2welcome offer, 15%
SHOOTHero: best-seller, styled simply
A single best-seller styled simply on-model, warm light, clean setting. Covetable and calm, the kind of frame that sells the piece before a word is read. If a slim countdown is used, it sits quietly at the edge and never shouts. A strong library frame can be reused here.
DYNAMIC FEEDBest-seller 2x2 product block
Pulled live from best-sellers. Four pieces, image and name, no prices.
Brief for designer · Row 6RO team: Brand-voiced push, not a founder letter, so no Elena portrait. Keep it premium, not a busy sale layout. Countdown timer is optional and should stay elegant.
E3why i made lohnr
CLIENT-SUPPLIEDElena founder portrait
A real, warm portrait of Elena, the founder. Natural light, relaxed, looking at or just past the camera. Personal rather than a polished campaign shot. This is what puts a face to the brand, so it has to genuinely be her. A model or a stock image would undo the whole point of the email.
Brief for designer · Row 6RO team: Client to supply the real photo. Editorial treatment, but keep the founder's personal register. The email ends on a single CTA to new arrivals.
E4the difference is in the fabric
SHOOTFabric close-up: Novella Set
An extreme close-up of the Novella Set's linen weave, shot so you can almost feel the slub of the linen and the small give of the stretch. Rake the light across the surface to catch the texture. High resolution, made to crop in tight.
SHOOTFabric close-up: Varsity Skort Set
A macro of the Varsity Skort's cotton blend, with a second frame revealing the built-in shorts under the skort. Show the detail most brands hide. Texture and construction both readable.
SHOOTFabric close-up: Ruched Set
A close shot of the Ruched Set's mid-weight fabric held or draped, so the weight and the clean fall come through. It should never look thin or sheer.
SHOOTFabric close-up: Calla Halter Dress
A macro of the Calla's smooth fabric catching light as it moves, showing the stretch and the way it holds its line.
SHOOTOptional on-model per piece
One motion frame per piece, worn and moving, to sit beneath each fabric close-up.
Brief for designer · Row 6RO team: Four summer best-sellers. Every fabric line is tied to the real product spec, so shoot the actual fabric, not a stand-in. Confirm the best-seller mix and keep it summer-relevant.
E5community / the inner circle
SHOOTCommunity hero
A candid, community-feel image. A few women together in a relaxed moment, more than one of them in Lohnr. It should feel caught, not cast. Warm and real.
SOURCE / UGCUGC grid: 4 to 6 photos
Four to six real customer photos from the UGC library. Women in their own homes and lives wearing Lohnr, phone-shot energy rather than studio. This is the proof that real people wear it.
SOURCE / UGCCustomer reviews (text)
Two or three real reviews pulled word-for-word from Shopify or Loox, first name and initial. Text, not an image. Nothing written by us.
Brief for designer · Row 6RO team: The Inner Circle CTA links to the Facebook page, where members apply and are approved, so supply that URL. No welcome-offer block here, the code has expired by this send.
