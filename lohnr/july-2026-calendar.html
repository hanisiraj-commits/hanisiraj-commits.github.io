The Plan
Campaign by Campaign
The month, send by send. The thing we’re watching most this time is who each email goes to. Sale and launch days go out wide. Teasers and features go to smaller, warmer segments, where they actually land.
Week 1Independence Day Sale
01 · FRI 3 JUL
Independence Day Sale Is Live
Promo · Elena founder letter · Full database
Open the long weekend with a plain-text letter from Elena. Personal, honest, no fake urgency. She names two or three in-season sets in the body: Novella, Roma, Clubhouse Skort.
Suggest 20% sitewide · code JULY4 · Fri 3 → Mon 6
Why it goes out wide: the sale launch is the biggest revenue moment of the month, and it earns most when it reaches the whole list. So this one goes to everyone.
02 · SAT 4 JUL
The Long Weekend Edit
Promo · Image editorial, single hero · Engaged 60-day
A visual push on the 4th itself, mid-sale. One hero set, styled for the long weekend. Not a multi-product roundup.
Hero: The Novella Set ($68) · CTA: Shop The Sale
Why: a single-hero image carries a sale better than a busy grid, and keeping it to the engaged segment holds the relevance high.
03 · MON 6 JUL
Independence Day Last Chance
Promo · Elena founder letter · Sale openers & non-purchasers
Close the sale tonight. Another letter from Elena, soft and personal. The sale really does end here, so last chance is the truth rather than a tactic.
20% ends tonight
Why: a personal close from the founder is one of the most dependable sends we have, and it gives the people on the fence a reason to move.
Week 2Varsity Skort Set Launch · Sat 11 Jul
04 · TUE 7 JUL
Varsity Skort Set: coming soon 👀
NPD 1 of 4 · Full-bleed tease · Engaged / waitlist-builder only, not the full DB
A stripped-back tease to build the waitlist. Full-bleed editorial hero, model in the set, in motion. One line of type, one CTA. The subject line carries an eye emoji, a deliberate one-off for this launch.
Hero: Varsity Skort Set ($75) · CTA: Join The Waitlist · Drop: Sat 11 Jul
Why narrow: a teaser doesn’t sell to a cold list, it just spends reach. So this one only goes to engaged openers, to build the waitlist. The selling happens on launch day.
05 · THU 9 JUL
introducing: the varsity skort set
NPD 2 of 4 · Image editorial reveal · Engaged + waitlist
The full reveal. Build runs hero, then a three-up detail grid, the silhouette / fabric / details blocks, a pocket close-up, a lifestyle scene, the “waitlist shops first on Saturday” block, then the CTA. No countdown timer. Any scarcity stays in the copy, and stays gentle.
Hero: Varsity Skort Set · CTA: Join The Waitlist · colour/size shown visually, never as text USPs
Why: the reveal does the convincing, so it widens out to the full engaged segment and builds the audience for Saturday.
06 · SAT 11 JUL · LAUNCH
it’s here. the varsity skort set
NPD 3 of 4 · Launch hero · Waitlist AM → full list midday
Launch day, two sends. In the morning, the waitlist gets it first: you’re in. At midday, the full list: it’s live. Full price throughout. The launch creative leads on the set, with the Clubhouse Skort Set ($58) alongside it as the companion.
Hero: Varsity Skort Set · Companion: Clubhouse Skort Set · CTA: Shop The Set
Why it goes out wide: a launch, like a sale, does its best numbers across the whole list. Letting the waitlist in first keeps the early-access promise without touching the price, and the companion skort lifts the basket.
07 · TUE 14 JUL
the set everyone’s reaching for
NPD 4 of 4 · Targeted second touch + social proof · Launch non-openers & engaged non-buyers
Pick up everyone who missed Saturday. New subject line, same hero, with a little early social proof once the first reactions land.
Audience: launch non-openers + engaged non-purchasers · CTA: Shop The Set
Why: a second, sharper send to a warm segment, the people who already opened or showed interest, usually beats the first wide swing.
Weeks 3–5Momentum & Feature
08 · SAT 18 JUL
why she keeps reaching for it
Feature · Single-product social proof · Engaged 60-day
A single-product spotlight carried by customer quotes. One hero set, real words from the people who own it.
Hero: The Roma Set · CTA: Shop The Set
Why: proof-led, single-product features are some of the best-converting emails we send. Roma is locked as the hero — it’s performing well in paid, so we carry that momentum into email.
09 · THU 23 JUL
the getaway collection: just landed
Feature · Getaway Collection arrivals, framed as a drop event · Engaged
A “just landed” Getaway Collection drop. Lead with the genuinely new pieces, and make sure the CTA matches what’s actually shown.
Showcase — newest arrivals from the Getaway Collection: Brennan Pant, Cleo Culotte, Oasis Pant, Cabana Button-Up · CTA: See What’s New
Why: arrivals do better gathered into one drop than dripped out across separate sends. And “See What’s New” only works when the heroes really are new, so we lead with this month’s pieces.
10 · TUE 28 JUL
how to style your summer sets
Content · Gabby / educational · Engaged & community
Keep the momentum going past launch week with a styling piece. A few ways to wear the Varsity Set and the Roma Set through summer, some community photos, and an invite to tag #LohnrLife.
Hero: Varsity Set + Roma Set styled three ways · CTA: Build Your Rotation
Why: it keeps the new launch and our best-performing set moving a couple of weeks on without another discount, and styling content is something we don’t lean on enough.
