The Plan
Ten sends grouped by week. Each campaign shows what the email is for, the products it features, and the strategic reasoning behind the slot.
Week One Summer Dress Setup + The Drop
01
Solene, Made for the Months Ahead
Tuesday 2 June · Dress Spotlight
Objective
Open the month on the brand’s hero dress. Reposition Solene from spring favourite to the summer dress in the wardrobe — the one you reach for through every kind of warm-weather day.
Products
Solene Dress (11 colours) · Cloud Modal Set as the companion piece
Why now: Solene has been the brand’s top revenue piece across April and May. Opening June on it sets the dress-led tone for the month and carries proven momentum into the new season.
02
Four New, Just Landed
Saturday 6 June · NPD Launch
Objective
Announce the full drop and bank a first-touch read on each piece. Lead the announcement with the Vivienne dress as the season’s statement headliner, with the rest of the release introduced underneath.
Products
Vivienne Bustier Dress (lead) · Bound Rib Set · Muse Shoulder Tee · Staple Scoop Tank
Why now: One brand-level moment to introduce the release together. Vivienne as the lead reinforces the dress-led theme of the month. Each product still gets its own block in the email so the drop reads as a release announcement.
Week Two Hero NPD Spotlights
03
Meet The Bound Rib Set
Tuesday 9 June · Product Spotlight
Objective
Establish the Bound Rib Set as the hero of the drop. Convert the early demand built by the Saturday launch and position the set as the standout non-dress piece of the release.
Products
Bound Rib Set · Solene Dress as the companion piece
Spotlight Angle — pick one
A · Recommended. "A clean coordinated look." Lead with the two-piece styling story — how it wears as a set or splits across the wardrobe.
B. Fabric-led: 230gsm ribbed knit, built for repeats.
C. Detail-led: lead with the contrast edge binding as the design hook.
Why now: Three days after the launch is the right window to give the highest-AOV piece its own moment while the introduction is still fresh. Solene as companion brings the proven seller into the frame as a natural cross-sell.
04
Vivienne, The Summer Dress
Friday 12 June · Dress Spotlight
Objective
Position Vivienne as the season’s statement-occasion dress. Capture weekend buying intent from customers shopping for something to wear to an event.
Products
Vivienne Bustier Dress · Solene Dress as the everyday companion to the statement piece
Spotlight Angle — pick one
A · Recommended. "The summer event piece." Lead with occasion-wear positioning — the dress you put on when something’s happening.
B. Denim reframe: lead with the unexpected denim build — "the dress version of your favourite jeans."
C. Silhouette-led: lead with the bustier shape as the new Lohnr summer silhouette.
Why now: Friday before a weekend creates the buying window for customers with something coming up. Second dress moment of the month, distinct from the everyday Solene angle — this one is occasion-led.
Week Three Elara Spotlight + Solstice Dress Sale Opens
05
Elara, Cotton in the Heat
Tuesday 16 June · Dress Spotlight
Objective
Give the Elara Pinafore its summer moment. Reposition the May launch from "100% cotton" to "the dress that breathes when nothing else will" — built for the season the customer is actually in right now.
Products
Elara Pinafore ($58, 6 colours, S-3XL, 100% cotton) · Solene as the companion dress
Why now: Elara has been pulling steadily since launch and the cotton story is the angle the customer base responded to. Sliding into the third week gives the dress its own moment ahead of the sale and keeps the dress-led cadence rolling. Sits between the NPD heroes and the sale window.
06
The Solstice Dress Sale, Open Now
Friday 19 June · Sale Launch
Objective
Open the sale on a dress-forward note. Sitewide eligibility, but the email leads with summer dresses as the call to action — the season’s reason to shop. Frame the moment as a seasonal dress reset, not a holiday hook.
Products
Vivienne, Solene, Elara called out by name · Bound Rib Set as the non-dress hero callout · Sitewide eligibility including the new arrivals
Sale Mechanics — needs sign-off
A · Recommended. 20% off sitewide, code SOLSTICE20, 19-22 June. Sitewide lever, dress-led email framing.
B. 25% off all dresses + 15% off everything else. Mechanical dress preference.
C. 15% off sitewide + extra 10% on new arrivals only.
Why now: Juneteenth, Solstice and Father’s Day land in the same weekend — the natural June retail moment. Dress-forward framing matches the month’s theme. Sitewide mechanic keeps optionality open for the customer.
07
The Solstice Dress Edit
Sunday 21 June · Sale Mid-Send
Objective
Catch the shoppers who saw Friday’s sale launch but haven’t bought yet. Surface the season’s dresses visually — Vivienne, Solene, Elara, Calypso Tiered — so the sale stays present through Father’s Day evening.
Products
Vivienne · Solene · Elara Pinafore · Calypso Tiered · Bound Rib Set as the non-dress slot
20% off sitewide · ends 22 June 11:59pm PT
Why now: Two-day gap from the open keeps the sale present without saturation. Lands on Father’s Day morning when households are browsing. Visual dress-led reminder reinforces the month’s theme inside the sale window.
Week Four Sale Close + Dress Edit + July Bridge
08
The Solstice Sale, Last Chance
Monday 22 June · Sale Close
Objective
Close the sale and capture the final wave of conversions. A personal, soft-urgency note from the founder that prompts hesitant shoppers without manufacturing panic. Dresses lead the product callouts.
Products
Top performers from the weekend — Vivienne, Solene, Elara most likely as the dress callouts
20% off sitewide · ends tonight 11:59pm PT
Why now: The founder-letter close has been the brand’s strongest sale-end format across the last three sale events. This is the template that owns the close.
09
The Summer Dress Edit
Thursday 25 June · Dress Edit
Objective
A curated dress moment matched to summer occasions. Each dress mapped to a specific kind of warm-weather day — everyday, event, heat, brunch, travel. Reads as category discovery within the dress world, not a styling roundup.
Products
Solene (everyday) · Vivienne (event) · Elara Pinafore (cotton heat) · Calypso Tiered (brunch/romantic) · Riviera Floral Maxi (travel)
Edit Composition — pick one
A · Recommended. Five dresses, each matched to a summer occasion. Best balance of breadth and focus.
B. Three dresses only (Solene, Vivienne, Elara), tighter focus. Lower fatigue risk.
C. Customer-led: dresses ranked by what’s actually moving across June. Live, data-driven.
Why now: Two days post-sale gives the audience breathing room and the dress edit reinforces the month’s theme. Different from a "build your wardrobe" roundup — this is single-category discovery, the way COS or Toteme run a Trouser Edit.
10
What’s Coming In July
Saturday 27 June · Community Letter
Objective
Bridge into July with a warm, brand-led moment. Reflect on the dresses that landed and moved in June, seed the July 4 sale ahead, and give the customer a soft touch that isn’t commerce-first. No offer attached.
Products
Light references to the dress edit standouts and the four NPDs · No new product feature
Slot Alternative — flexible
A · Recommended. Community letter with a July 4 sale tease. Warms without giving mechanics away.
B. Review drive: TrustPilot ask with the $50-off-next-order incentive. Repeat of May’s play — second pass within 30 days may be premature.
C. Subtle Pride acknowledgment + brand values letter.
Why now: The post-sale window is the right place for a brand-voice moment. Builds the bridge into July without sending another commerce-heavy email so soon after the Solstice close.
