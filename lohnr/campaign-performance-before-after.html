Insights & Talking Points

Before (18 Nov 2025–7 Mar 2026) vs After (8 Mar–26 Jun 2026) · equal 110-day windows · Klaviyo Placed Order attribution
Revenue and orders doubled — on fewer sends
Campaign-attributed revenue rose +103% ($16,577 → $33,611) and orders +114% (175 → 375), while sending 15% fewer campaigns. Revenue per send jumped +140% ($425 → $1,019) — each email works harder, not just sends more.
Revenue doubled in an off-peak window
The earlier period included Black Friday & Cyber Monday — the year's biggest sale moment — and was still doubled across an ordinary Spring/Summer stretch. That earlier BFCM push was 23 sends for just $1,845 (~$80/send); volume without precision.
The list is healthier, not burnt
Bounce rate fell 87% (1.36% → 0.18%) and unsubscribes 29%, even while doubling reach to 688K. More people, reached more cleanly — protecting long-term deliverability instead of spending it.
The open/click dip is a scale effect, not a decline
Open (60% → 51%) and click (0.34% → 0.24%) rates eased — but those earlier rates sat on a list half the size with a bounce rate 7× higher. Reach and revenue both doubled while conversion rate held (+3.5%). Rate dilution from healthy expansion, not lost performance.
The next lever: click efficiency
Click rate is the account's true ceiling. The plain-text Elena founder letters convert the click best (IWD follow-up hit 0.70% — 3× average), and tighter segmentation on non-promo sends has out-earned broad blasts 2–3× on RPR. That's where the next revenue step-change comes from.
